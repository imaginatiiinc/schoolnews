NOTES FROM THE PRINCIPAL
We look forward to seeing many sophomores, juniors, and seniors tonight at the Winter Formal at Newport Syndicate (18 E. 5th St., Newport, KY 41071). Dances like this one are important for our students as they celebrate their school community.


We join you in wanting the evening to be safe and enjoyable for all of our guests. One of the biggest challenges of parenting teenagers is balancing the line between trusting our children and keeping them safe. Since we are all members of the Saint Ursula community, let’s continue to keep the health and safety of our girls a priority.


If you are hosting students at your house after the dance, be an active chaperone while there are guests in your home. If your daughter is going to someone else’s house after the dance, feel free to call the parents who are hosting to verify that adults will be home. Let’s support each other as we raise our daughter to be thinkers, leaders, nurturers, and prophets.


Please join Lelia and me in congratulating Kellie Hannan, Class of 2020. Kellie is the lead author of a national paper on the effects of Racial Sympathy as it relates to Capital Punishment. She will be presenting the findings of her team at the American Society of Criminology at the Marriott Marquis in San Francisco November 17. Following the conference, Kellie’s paper will be published in the American Journal of Criminology.


Additionally, please pray for our six students and two teachers who will be traveling to Washington, D.C. for the Ignatian Family Teach-In November 15-18. During their time at the Family Teach-In, this group will learn about being advocates for change!

Mari Thomas, Principal
mthomas@saintursula.org
